ShipBoost setup guide

Get your free shipping progress bar live in a few minutes. Everything is managed from the ShipBoost admin and your Shopify theme editor — no theme code required.

Installation

  1. Install ShipBoost from the Shopify App Store and open the app from your Shopify admin.
  2. Open Settings and set your free shipping goal amount.
  3. Choose a template and adjust colors, typography and border radius to match your brand.
  4. Set placement and visibility — which pages, the position, and mobile or desktop.
  5. Save your settings, then add the app block to your theme (next section).

Adding the theme app block

ShipBoost renders through a Shopify theme app block, so it is added visually in your theme editor and never edits your theme code.

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Online Store → Themes and click Customize.
  2. Navigate to a product page template (or the page/section where you want the bar).
  3. Click Add block (or Add section) and choose ShipBoost Progress Bar under the Apps group.
  4. Position the block where you would like it, then click Save.
  5. Preview your storefront to confirm the bar appears.
Tip: the block must be added on each template where you want the bar to appear. The Position setting then fine-tunes exactly where it sits relative to your product content.

Configuring the progress bar

All of the following are controlled from the ShipBoost Settings page:

General

  • Enable progress bar — the master on/off switch.
  • Free shipping goal — the cart total that unlocks free shipping.
  • Remaining message — shown below the goal. Use the {{remaining}} token to insert the amount left to spend.
  • Success message — shown once the customer qualifies.

Appearance

  • Template style — Modern, Glass, Gradient, Minimal, Luxury or Neon.
  • Colors — progress bar, background and success colors.
  • Border radius and bar height.

Typography

  • Font family — a Google Font, or Theme Default to inherit your store font.
  • Font size, weight, text color and alignment.

Placement & visibility

  • Display on — which storefront pages show the bar.
  • Position — where the bar appears (see below).
  • Enable on mobile / desktop — device visibility.

Placement options

The Position setting places the bar relative to standard product-page elements:

  • Above Product Information — at the top of the product details column.
  • Below Product Information — beneath the product details column.
  • Above Add to Cart Button — directly above the Add to Cart button.
  • Below Add to Cart Button — directly below the Add to Cart button (highest visibility).

The bar aligns to the same width and spacing as your Add to Cart button. If the chosen spot doesn’t exist in your theme, the bar simply stays where you placed the block — it never breaks your storefront.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to edit my theme code?
No. ShipBoost is a theme app block added visually in the theme editor. It never modifies your theme files.
Which themes are supported?
Any Shopify Online Store 2.0 theme that supports app blocks, including Dawn and Horizon.
Will it slow down my store?
No. ShipBoost ships a small stylesheet and script, reads the cart from the storefront, and only loads the single Google Font you select (Theme Default loads none).
Does the bar update as customers add items?
Yes. It reads the live cart and re-renders whenever the cart changes, including a success state once the goal is reached.
Can I customize the message?
Yes. Edit both the remaining and success messages in Settings. Use {{remaining}} to insert the amount left to reach free shipping.
Does ShipBoost collect customer data?
No. ShipBoost reads the cart total on the storefront to calculate progress and does not store customer personal data. See our Privacy Policy.

Troubleshooting

The bar doesn’t appear

  • Confirm the progress bar is enabled in Settings.
  • Confirm the ShipBoost block has been added in your theme editor.
  • Check that the page you’re viewing matches your Display on setting.
  • Check that the current device (mobile/desktop) is enabled.

It shows in the wrong place

  • Review your Position setting.
  • Make sure the block is added on the matching page template.

The font looks wrong

  • Theme Default always uses your store’s font. Re-save Settings to reload the selected font.

Text is hard to read

  • Adjust the Text color in Settings — the live preview warns when contrast against your background is low.
